You just think...(History)

Since 1954 the Choreography (Aereography) for Peter Pan The Musical has remained relatively consistent. While each production and each Flying Director brings their own flair to the production, the basic movements are based entirely on the version created by the father of Theatrical Flying Effects, Peter Foy.

...Lovely Wonderful Thoughts... (The Set)

The Nursery is the scene of the main flying event. Because of this, and the consistent structure of the Aereography, all Nursery sets must contain a few Key Elements.

  • A Window without a top located Upstage Center
  • A Fireplace Mantle located downstage near directly below the Flying equipment
  • Another Structural object located opposite the mantle
With these pieces (plus any additional pieces needed for the scene) you can create the basic structure of the standard flying effect for "I'm Flying", which introduces Peter Pan's magical ability to the audience and concludes the first Act. (The big number)

...And UP YOU GO! (The Aereography)

To simplify the explanation of the movements, we will make a few assumptions:

  • The Mantle is on Stage Right
  • There is a bed on Stage Left
  • There are no obstructions between the mantle and the bed
Here is a brief description of the steps per sequence of "I'm Flying". The Cue line is numbered and it's steps sub-listed:
  1. ..And up you go
    • Peter goes straight up and waits a beat
    • Travels away from the Mantle until the Start of the song
    • Some number of passes back and forth to land on "..Trying"
  2. High Up and as light...
    • Peter goes up by some alternative means (climbing a ladder or swimming up) on 3 "bumps"
    • Travel away form mantle and make some number of passes (depending on the size of the stage)
    • Land on bed on the line "..way up off the ground."
  3. I Fly and I'm...
    • Peter leaps from Bed to Mantle
    • Magical Lean off Mantle and back to standing
  4. I'm Flying! Over bed over chair...
    • Some number of passes (depending on stage)
    • Land on stage off of center
This concludes the "I'm Flying" song. After this song, Pan blows fairy dust and the children and teaches the kids to fly. The Act concludes with Peter, Wendy, Michael, and John all flying while the set is cleared in view.
